Market Overview

A market finding its footing

After four years of strong price growth, Singapore's HDB resale market settled into a notably calmer pace in 2025.

The HDB resale market posted its slowest price growth since 2019, with the Resale Price Index rising just 2.9% for the full year and hitting 0% in Q4 — the first flat quarter since 2020. The overall median resale price reached S$628,000, while total transaction volume dipped 9.7% to 25,092 units, constrained by the lowest MOP flat supply in eleven years. Paradoxically, million-dollar transactions surged to a record 1,594 deals, up 54% from 2024, reflecting the persistent premium on well-located, newer flats in mature estates like the Central Area, Queenstown, and Toa Payoh.

Market Narrative

Three trends shaping the market

What the data says about where the market has been — and where it's going.

Trend 01
📊
Price Growth Slowing to a Plateau

HDB resale prices grew just 2.9% in 2025, the slowest annual growth since 2019. Q4 registered 0% quarterly growth — the first flat quarter in five years. Buyers are increasingly price-resistant, especially in mid and lower-end segments, with only 8 of 26 towns recording median transactions above S$750K.

🎯 ERA forecasts 2–5% growth in 2026
🔄
Trend 02
Volume Cooling, Then Rebounding

Full-year 2025 volume fell 9.7% to 26,169 units, driven by the lowest MOP flat supply in eleven years (6,973 units) and competing SBF exercises. Q4 2025 hit the weakest quarterly level since Q2 2020. But Q1 2026 bounced back to 6,285 transactions (+20% QoQ) — buyers re-enter when prices stop climbing.

✅ Q1 2026: +20% QoQ rebound
💎
Trend 03
Million-Dollar Flats & Foreign Buyer Retreat

Million-dollar HDB transactions hit a record 1,594 in 2025, up 54% from 1,035 in 2024 — over half involving flats aged 15 years or younger in mature estates. Meanwhile the private market is cooling at the top: ABSD tightening in Dec 2025 has squeezed foreign buyers to just 4.2% of private purchases in Q1 2026, down from 7.8%.

⚡ Foreign share: 7.8% → 4.2%
